Week 8

During the Health and Economics panel at Talking Economics Ben Moll said that consumer spending in Sweden fell by almost as much as Denmark despite the later having a ‘harder’ lockdown. The lockdowns in both countries were originally introduced between January and April of 2020. Where Denmark’s consumption actually fell by less compared to Sweden, and despite Denmark having an additional ‘weaker’ lockdown in December 2020 we can see that consumption quickly bounces back regardless of lockdown strength and that there are other important factors than lockdown on consumption expenditure.

Regression between world stock price and total worldwide cases, regression is done inside of the vega-lite file

These graphs show regressions of the total covid cases world wide against the closing stock price of the exchange traded fund VTI which is a Vanguard 'stock' which tracks the tota value of the entire worlds stock market. It's the easiest and most accepted way of tracking the value of the entire worlds stock market as if they were all part of one singular market. The covid data is from Johns Hopkins University and the VTI stock data is from yahoo finance
